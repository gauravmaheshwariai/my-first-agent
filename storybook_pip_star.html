The Little Star Who Was Scared of the Dark

Up in the night sky, there was a little star named Pip. All the other stars were twinkling and shining. But Pip just sat there, hiding behind a cloud.

You see, Pip was a little bit scared. The sky was SO big and SO dark. What if nobody could see such a tiny little star?

Then a big, warm voice said, 'Hello, little one.' It was Mama Moon, floating slowly by. She had the kindest, softest face Pip had ever seen.

'I'm too small,' said Pip. 'Nobody needs me.' Mama Moon just smiled and shook her head slowly. 'Oh, Pip,' she said. 'Someone is waiting for exactly your light.'

Pip peeked out just a tiny bit. And way, way down below, a little child was lying in bed, looking up at the window. The child smiled. 'There's my star,' the child whispered.

Pip shone as bright as could be, all night long. And every night after that, Pip was never scared anymore. Because Pip knew — somewhere down below, someone was looking up and smiling.

You don't have to be the biggest to matter — someone out there is glad you're here.
