Krishi and the Sleepy Stars

It was nighttime, and Krishi could not sleep. She crept outside and sat on the big old tree branch in her backyard. The moon was round and soft and glowing just for her.

Then she heard a soft sound. Hoo... hoo... Sitting right beside her was a big fluffy owl with tiny little glasses! He smiled a slow, wise smile. 'Hello, little one,' he said. 'You are up very late.'

'I cannot sleep,' said Krishi. 'My brain keeps thinking and thinking and thinking!' Ottu nodded slowly, like he had heard this a hundred times before. 'Ah yes,' he said. 'Your brain needs a little help to rest.'

Ottu pointed one soft wing up at the sky. 'Look at those stars,' he whispered. 'Even the stars get tired and yawn at night.' Krishi looked up and giggled, because she thought she saw one tiny star blink slowly, just like a sleepy eye.

Ottu spoke in a low, slow, sleepy voice. 'The flowers are asleep. The birds are asleep. Even the little ants are tucked in tight.' Krishi felt her eyes go heavy, just a little bit heavy. She leaned her head against Ottu's soft, fluffy feathers.

Ottu whispered one last thing before she drifted off. 'You are safe, little one. The night is just the world giving you a hug.' Krishi smiled a soft, small smile. And just like that, she was ready to sleep.

The night is not something to be afraid of — it is just the world wrapping you up in a great big cozy hug.
